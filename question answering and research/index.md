# Role-Based Question Answering and Research Prompts

## QA (Quality Assurance)

1. "Answer this QA question about [topic] clearly, including assumptions, examples, and how it applies to real testing work."
2. "Research best practices for testing [feature/system type] and summarize practical recommendations for a QA team."
3. "Explain the difference between unit, integration, regression, smoke, sanity, and UAT testing with examples for [product]."
4. "Find likely causes of [testing problem] and summarize how QA teams usually diagnose and resolve it."
5. "Answer whether [tool/approach] is appropriate for our QA process, considering team size, skill, cost, and maintenance."
6. "Research common failure modes in [domain/system] and translate them into testing focus areas."
7. "Explain how to measure QA effectiveness for [team/product] without relying only on test case counts."
8. "Answer this question from a stakeholder: [question], using plain language and explaining testing implications."
9. "Research approaches for improving test data management and summarize options, trade-offs, and prerequisites."
10. "Explain how QA should participate in requirements review for [project], including questions to ask and artifacts to produce."

## Business Analysts

1. "Answer this business analysis question about [topic] with definitions, examples, and practical implications."
2. "Research common approaches to requirements elicitation for [project type] and summarize the most useful methods."
3. "Explain [business concept/process] in plain language for stakeholders with different levels of domain knowledge."
4. "Answer what information a BA should gather before starting [initiative], grouped by business, user, data, process, and technical context."
5. "Research industry examples of [business capability/process] and summarize patterns, risks, and lessons learned."
6. "Explain the difference between business requirements, functional requirements, non-functional requirements, and acceptance criteria."
7. "Answer stakeholder questions about [project/change], separating known facts, assumptions, unknowns, and next research steps."
8. "Research techniques for process mapping and recommend which to use for [process/context]."
9. "Explain how to evaluate whether [solution] solves [business problem], including evidence and success metrics."
10. "Answer this RFP or discovery question [question] with a structured, evidence-oriented response."

## Developers

1. "Answer this technical question about [topic] with a concise explanation, example, trade-offs, and common pitfalls."
2. "Research implementation options for [feature] and summarize recommended approaches for a developer starting the work."
3. "Explain how [technology/API/pattern] works using an example relevant to [project/context]."
4. "Answer why [error/behavior] may be happening, listing likely causes and how to verify each one."
5. "Research libraries or tools for [task] and compare them by maturity, fit, complexity, and maintenance risk."
6. "Explain the security considerations for implementing [feature], including common vulnerabilities and mitigations."
7. "Answer this codebase onboarding question [question] by explaining the likely concepts, files, and flows to inspect."
8. "Research database design patterns for [use case] and summarize practical trade-offs."
9. "Explain how to test [feature/module] effectively, covering unit, integration, contract, and end-to-end tests."
10. "Answer this deployment or CI/CD question [question] with likely causes, checks, and corrective actions."

## Senior Developers

1. "Answer this architecture question about [system/decision] with trade-offs, constraints, risks, and recommended next steps."
2. "Research architectural patterns for [problem] and summarize when each is appropriate."
3. "Explain [complex technical concept] to both engineers and non-engineering stakeholders."
4. "Answer whether we should adopt [technology], considering strategy, team capability, ecosystem maturity, cost, and migration risk."
5. "Research reliability practices for [system type] and summarize concrete changes we could make."
6. "Explain the operational implications of [architecture/design], including monitoring, incident response, scaling, and ownership."
7. "Answer this engineering leadership question [question] with a balanced view of delivery, quality, people, and risk."
8. "Research migration strategies from [current system] to [target system] and summarize viable paths."
9. "Explain how to evaluate [technical initiative] using leading indicators, lagging indicators, and guardrail metrics."
10. "Answer this incident-related question [question] by separating facts, hypotheses, evidence, and systemic lessons."

## Product People

1. "Answer this product question about [topic] with user impact, business context, evidence, and next decision."
2. "Research market trends for [space] and summarize implications for [product/company]."
3. "Explain [product concept/framework] in practical terms, with examples and when to use it."
4. "Answer whether we should build [feature] by considering user need, strategy, evidence, effort, risk, and alternatives."
5. "Research competitors for [product/category] and summarize positioning, feature gaps, pricing signals, and differentiation."
6. "Explain what we need to learn before committing to [initiative], including research questions and evidence sources."
7. "Answer stakeholder questions about roadmap priority [item] using transparent reasoning and known constraints."
8. "Research user behavior around [problem area] and summarize likely motivations, barriers, and opportunities."
9. "Explain how to measure success for [feature/launch], including primary, secondary, and guardrail metrics."
10. "Answer this go-to-market or launch question [question] with cross-functional considerations and open risks."
