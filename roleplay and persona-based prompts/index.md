# Role-Based Roleplay and Persona-Based Prompts

## QA (Quality Assurance)

1. "Act as a skeptical QA lead reviewing [feature] and challenge the test coverage, risk assumptions, and release readiness."
2. "Act as an end user trying to complete [workflow] and describe what feels confusing, broken, risky, or unexpected."
3. "Act as a developer receiving this bug report [report] and explain what information is missing before you can reproduce it."
4. "Act as a release manager and question whether [release] is ready based on open defects, test evidence, and rollback options."
5. "Act as a UAT participant for [feature] and provide realistic feedback about usability, business fit, and acceptance blockers."
6. "Act as a production support analyst reviewing [incident] and identify what QA signals could have detected it earlier."
7. "Act as a test automation architect and critique this automation approach [approach] for brittleness, maintainability, and value."
8. "Act as a compliance auditor reviewing QA evidence for [release] and identify gaps in traceability, sign-off, and documentation."
9. "Act as a frustrated customer affected by [defect] and explain the real-world impact in plain language."
10. "Act as a bug triage facilitator and lead a discussion about severity, priority, ownership, and next action for [defect]."

## Business Analysts

1. "Act as a stakeholder in [department] and answer discovery questions about goals, pain points, constraints, and success criteria."
2. "Act as a skeptical executive reviewing [project] and challenge the business case, scope, assumptions, and expected outcomes."
3. "Act as an operations user walking through [process] and describe current-state workarounds, delays, and failure points."
4. "Act as a workshop facilitator and guide stakeholders through resolving conflicting requirements for [initiative]."
5. "Act as a system owner and explain data, integration, compliance, and process constraints affecting [project]."
6. "Act as a customer service representative using [workflow] and identify missing requirements from the front-line perspective."
7. "Act as a change manager and critique the adoption risks, training needs, and communication plan for [change]."
8. "Act as a procurement reviewer and question this RFP response [response] for clarity, evidence, compliance, and differentiation."
9. "Act as a process mapping participant and narrate each step, handoff, exception, and pain point in [process]."
10. "Act as a decision owner and evaluate whether these requirements [requirements] are sufficient to approve delivery."

## Developers

1. "Act as a senior code reviewer and review [code/change] for correctness, maintainability, security, performance, and test coverage."
2. "Act as a new developer onboarding to [codebase] and ask the questions needed to understand structure, build, data flow, and first task."
3. "Act as a production debugger investigating [issue] and walk through hypotheses, evidence, instrumentation, and next checks."
4. "Act as an API consumer using [endpoint] and critique request shape, response shape, errors, docs, and developer experience."
5. "Act as a security reviewer and identify abuse cases, permission issues, data exposure risks, and mitigation gaps in [feature]."
6. "Act as a database administrator reviewing [schema/query] and critique correctness, indexing, migrations, and operational risk."
7. "Act as a QA engineer testing [feature] and identify edge cases, test data needs, and likely defect areas."
8. "Act as a product manager reading this technical design [design] and ask clarifying questions about user impact and trade-offs."
9. "Act as a site reliability engineer reviewing [service] and challenge its observability, failure handling, and deploy safety."
10. "Act as a pair-programming partner and guide me through implementing [feature] one decision at a time."

## Senior Developers

1. "Act as an architecture review board member and challenge [design] across scalability, security, reliability, cost, and operability."
2. "Act as a principal engineer mentoring a team through [technical initiative] and give feedback on sequencing, ownership, and risk."
3. "Act as an incident commander during [incident] and guide the team through triage, communication, mitigation, and follow-up."
4. "Act as a skeptical platform owner reviewing adoption of [technology] and ask hard questions about maturity, support, and exit cost."
5. "Act as an executive sponsor and ask what business value, risk reduction, and measurable outcomes justify [technical initiative]."
6. "Act as a migration lead planning [migration] and pressure-test rollback, data validation, dependencies, and communication."
7. "Act as an engineering manager reviewing a technical debt proposal [proposal] and challenge prioritization, scope, and evidence."
8. "Act as a reliability expert and critique how [system] behaves under dependency failure, load spikes, deploy mistakes, and data corruption."
9. "Act as a cross-team technical lead and facilitate alignment on [decision], including decision rights, objections, and next steps."
10. "Act as a postmortem reviewer and evaluate whether [postmortem] identifies systemic causes and action items that will prevent recurrence."

## Product People

1. "Act as a target user for [feature] and react honestly to the value proposition, workflow, friction, and missing capabilities."
2. "Act as a skeptical customer buyer evaluating [product] and ask about ROI, trust, implementation effort, support, and alternatives."
3. "Act as a sales representative preparing to sell [feature] and identify objections, proof points, and enablement gaps."
4. "Act as a support lead reviewing a launch plan [plan] and flag likely customer confusion, policy gaps, and training needs."
5. "Act as a product critic and challenge whether [feature] solves a real problem or only adds complexity."
6. "Act as an executive stakeholder reviewing [roadmap] and question strategic fit, trade-offs, timing, and measurable outcomes."
7. "Act as a user researcher interviewing a customer about [problem area], asking neutral questions and probing for behavior."
8. "Act as a competitor's product manager and explain how you would position against [product/feature]."
9. "Act as a growth product manager and critique [funnel/experiment] for hypothesis quality, metric choice, and decision rule."
10. "Act as a go-to-market lead preparing [launch] and identify messaging, segmentation, readiness, and measurement gaps."
