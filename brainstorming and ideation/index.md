# Role-Based Brainstorming and Ideation Prompts

## QA (Quality Assurance)

1. "Brainstorm test scenarios for [feature], including common paths, edge cases, failure modes, and unexpected user behavior."
2. "Generate creative ways to find defects in [system] beyond the documented happy path."
3. "Brainstorm risk areas for [release] by thinking through integrations, data changes, permissions, performance, and user workflows."
4. "Generate ideas for improving our automated test coverage for [module] without creating brittle or high-maintenance tests."
5. "Brainstorm negative test cases for this user story: [story], including invalid inputs, missing data, race conditions, and permission issues."
6. "Generate a list of exploratory testing charters for [feature], each focused on a different risk or user behavior."
7. "Brainstorm ways QA can detect regressions earlier in the development cycle for [team/process]."
8. "Generate ideas for improving bug reports so developers can reproduce and fix issues faster."
9. "Brainstorm quality metrics for [product/team] that would give useful signal without encouraging shallow test activity."
10. "Generate ideas for stress-testing [system] under unusual load, degraded dependencies, partial outages, and bad data."

## Business Analysts

1. "Brainstorm stakeholder interview questions for [project] that uncover goals, pain points, constraints, and success criteria."
2. "Generate possible business requirements for [initiative], grouped by user need, operational need, reporting need, and compliance need."
3. "Brainstorm current-state process pain points for [process] from the perspectives of users, managers, operations, and customers."
4. "Generate ideas for workshop activities that would help stakeholders align on scope, priorities, and decision rights for [project]."
5. "Brainstorm assumptions behind this business goal: [goal], then list ways to validate or challenge each one."
6. "Generate possible acceptance criteria for this capability: [capability], including happy path, exceptions, data rules, and non-functional needs."
7. "Brainstorm data elements we may need to capture for [workflow], including source systems, owners, definitions, and quality risks."
8. "Generate ideas for improving handoff between business analysis, design, development, QA, and operations for [project]."
9. "Brainstorm alternative solution approaches for [business problem], ranging from process changes to automation to system replacement."
10. "Generate a list of risks, dependencies, and open questions that should be resolved before committing to [initiative]."

## Developers

1. "Brainstorm implementation approaches for [feature], including simple, scalable, incremental, and low-risk options."
2. "Generate possible edge cases for [function/API/workflow] that should influence the design and tests."
3. "Brainstorm ways to simplify this code or architecture: [description], focusing on fewer moving parts and clearer ownership."
4. "Generate ideas for debugging [issue] when reproduction is inconsistent and logs are incomplete."
5. "Brainstorm database schema options for [feature], including trade-offs around query patterns, constraints, migrations, and future changes."
6. "Generate API design alternatives for [new endpoint], including request shape, response shape, validation, errors, and backward compatibility."
7. "Brainstorm potential failure modes for [service/module] and ways the code could detect, recover, or degrade gracefully."
8. "Generate ideas for improving local developer setup for [repo/project] so new contributors can build, test, and debug faster."
9. "Brainstorm refactoring opportunities in [module] that would reduce duplication, clarify data flow, or make tests easier."
10. "Generate possible names for functions, classes, events, database tables, or concepts related to [feature/domain]."

## Senior Developers

1. "Brainstorm architectural options for [system], including monolith, modular monolith, services, event-driven, and hybrid approaches."
2. "Generate technical strategy ideas for reducing [problem], such as latency, incidents, cloud cost, build time, or operational toil."
3. "Brainstorm migration paths from [old system] to [new system] that preserve user experience and allow rollback."
4. "Generate possible RFC angles for [technical decision], including goals, non-goals, alternatives, risks, and adoption concerns."
5. "Brainstorm ways to improve engineering quality for [team], covering code review, testing, observability, documentation, and ownership."
6. "Generate ideas for making [system] more resilient to dependency outages, traffic spikes, bad deploys, and data corruption."
7. "Brainstorm technical debt themes across [codebase/team], then group them by user impact, operational risk, and developer productivity."
8. "Generate mentoring project ideas that would help an engineer grow from [current level] to [target level]."
9. "Brainstorm cross-team alignment mechanisms for [initiative], including RFCs, design reviews, migration guides, dashboards, and office hours."
10. "Generate ideas for measuring the success of [technical initiative] with leading indicators, lagging indicators, and guardrail metrics."

## Product People

1. "Brainstorm product opportunities for [problem area], grouped by user segment, pain point, business value, and feasibility."
2. "Generate feature ideas for [product] that could improve activation, retention, monetization, or customer satisfaction."
3. "Brainstorm customer research questions for [hypothesis] that avoid leading the participant and reveal real behavior."
4. "Generate possible positioning angles for [product/feature] across different audiences, jobs-to-be-done, and competitive alternatives."
5. "Brainstorm experiments to validate [hypothesis], ranging from lightweight research to prototypes to A/B tests."
6. "Generate launch campaign ideas for [feature], including internal enablement, beta users, messaging, support, and analytics."
7. "Brainstorm roadmap themes for [product] over the next [timeframe], connecting each theme to outcomes and evidence."
8. "Generate ideas for improving a funnel step where users drop off at [step], including UX, messaging, timing, trust, and performance factors."
9. "Brainstorm pricing, packaging, or plan-tier ideas for [product], including customer value, willingness to pay, and operational complexity."
10. "Generate possible success metrics for [initiative], including primary metrics, secondary metrics, qualitative signals, and guardrails."
