# Role-Based Analysis and Reasoning Prompts

## QA (Quality Assurance)

1. "Analyze the risk profile of [feature/release], considering complexity, user impact, integrations, data changes, and historical defects."
2. "Compare manual, automated, and exploratory testing approaches for [feature], including pros, cons, costs, and coverage gaps."
3. "Evaluate this defect pattern [pattern] and identify likely root causes across requirements, development, testing, and release process."
4. "Analyze whether [release] is ready to ship based on test completion, defect severity, regression risk, and business urgency."
5. "Reason through why this intermittent failure [failure] may occur, listing hypotheses and evidence needed for each."
6. "Evaluate this test strategy [strategy] for completeness, maintainability, risk alignment, and blind spots."
7. "Compare severity and priority for these defects [defects], explaining the reasoning behind each classification."
8. "Analyze production defect leakage for [period] and identify process improvements that would reduce recurrence."
9. "Evaluate the trade-offs of increasing test automation for [module] versus improving exploratory testing and test data quality."
10. "Perform a SWOT analysis of the current QA process for [team/product]."

## Business Analysts

1. "Analyze this business problem [problem] by separating symptoms, root causes, stakeholders, constraints, and decision points."
2. "Compare solution options for [initiative], including cost, value, risk, dependencies, user impact, and implementation complexity."
3. "Evaluate this requirement [requirement] for ambiguity, testability, business value, feasibility, and missing assumptions."
4. "Analyze the current-state process [process] and identify bottlenecks, rework loops, control gaps, and automation opportunities."
5. "Reason through dependencies between these capabilities [capabilities] and recommend an implementation order."
6. "Evaluate stakeholder positions on [decision] by comparing incentives, concerns, influence, and likely objections."
7. "Analyze this RFP requirement set [requirements] and identify compliance risks, evidence gaps, and response strategy."
8. "Compare build, buy, and partner options for [business need] using a clear decision framework."
9. "Evaluate this proposed process change [change] for operational impact, training needs, adoption risk, and measurement approach."
10. "Perform a root-cause analysis for why [business metric] is underperforming."

## Developers

1. "Analyze this bug [bug] by identifying likely root causes, reproduction paths, affected components, and validation steps."
2. "Compare implementation options for [feature], including complexity, maintainability, performance, security, and delivery risk."
3. "Evaluate this code design [design] for cohesion, coupling, testability, extensibility, and failure handling."
4. "Reason through the data flow for [workflow] and identify where state, validation, or permissions may break."
5. "Analyze this performance issue [issue] by proposing hypotheses, measurements, experiments, and likely bottlenecks."
6. "Compare database approaches for [use case], including normalized tables, denormalization, indexes, caching, and event storage."
7. "Evaluate the trade-offs of introducing [library/tool] into this project [project]."
8. "Analyze this API design [api] for developer experience, backward compatibility, validation, error behavior, and observability."
9. "Reason through how this change [change] could fail in production and what safeguards should exist."
10. "Perform a root-cause analysis for this failed deployment [deployment]."

## Senior Developers

1. "Analyze architectural options for [system] and compare them across scalability, reliability, cost, team ownership, and migration risk."
2. "Evaluate this technical strategy [strategy] for coherence, sequencing, dependencies, operational readiness, and measurable outcomes."
3. "Reason through the trade-offs of migrating from [current architecture] to [target architecture]."
4. "Analyze this incident [incident] across detection, response, contributing factors, systemic causes, and prevention."
5. "Compare short-term mitigation and long-term redesign options for [technical problem]."
6. "Evaluate this RFC [rfc] for clarity of goals, decision quality, alternatives considered, risks, and rollout plan."
7. "Analyze technical debt in [area] by separating customer impact, delivery drag, operational risk, and architectural constraint."
8. "Reason through capacity planning for [system], including expected growth, bottlenecks, SLOs, and cost trade-offs."
9. "Evaluate whether [new technology] is appropriate for our stack, considering maturity, team expertise, ecosystem, and exit cost."
10. "Perform a SWOT analysis of the engineering organization or platform supporting [product]."

## Product People

1. "Analyze this product opportunity [opportunity] by evaluating user need, market size, strategic fit, feasibility, and risk."
2. "Compare roadmap options for [product], including expected outcomes, dependencies, cost of delay, and confidence."
3. "Evaluate this product hypothesis [hypothesis] for clarity, falsifiability, evidence strength, and experiment design."
4. "Reason through why [metric] changed, separating acquisition, activation, retention, monetization, and external factors."
5. "Analyze customer feedback [feedback] to identify themes, contradictions, evidence quality, and product implications."
6. "Compare target segments for [product], including pain intensity, willingness to pay, reachability, and competitive dynamics."
7. "Evaluate this launch plan [plan] for readiness, risk, cross-functional alignment, and measurement quality."
8. "Analyze the pros and cons of building [feature] now versus deferring it."
9. "Reason through the likely adoption barriers for [feature] and what evidence would validate or disprove them."
10. "Perform a SWOT analysis of [product] in its current market."
